Camellia Debnath

Data Scientist II @ Amazon

I am currently working as a Data Scientist at Amazon. I graduated from The Khoury College of Computer Sciences at Northeastern Univerisity, majoring in Data Science. I was a Spring'20 Graduate Research Fellow at Khoury, and also worked as a Teaching Assistant for the graduate level course CS5800 Algorithms.

At Northeastern, I was a part of Dr Karl Lieberherr's research team at Northeastern, working on Neural Monte Carlo Tree Search and its various applications, especially on combinatorial optimization problems. In 2019, I interned at Fidelity Investments, where I created the Market Risk Impact application, consisting of a deep reinforcement learning model to simulate market scenarios. During 2015-18, I worked at Citicorp as a full-stack developer, where I worked in the Capital Markets Technology team, developing and maintaining an Automated Test Generation Engine for converting plaintext testing requirements to JUnit testcases using Natural Language Processing. I also worked on a Brokerage Fee prediction model, and real time processing of Big Data for auditing purposes.

At Citicorp I was a part of Prometheus, a global leadership development program. During my undergrad, I volunteered as a teacher for school children for the National Service Scheme (NSS), an Indian government-sponsored public service program. During my spare time, I like reading, sketching and more recently, going on hikes near Seattle.
