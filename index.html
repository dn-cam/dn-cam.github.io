Camellia Debnath

MS in Data Science (August 20), Khoury College of Computer Sciences, Northeastern University

Camellia Debnath is a graduate student at Khoury College of Computer Sciences at Northeastern Univerisity, majoring in Data Science. She is also a Spring'20 Graduate Research Fellow at Khoury, and currently working as a Teaching Assistant for the graduate level course CS5800 Algorithms.

Currently Camellia is a part of Dr Karl Lieberherr's research team at Northeastern, working on Neural Monte Carlo Tree Search and its various applications, especially on combinatorial optimization problems. In 2019, she interened at Fidelity Investments, where she created the Market Risk Impact application, consisting of a deep reinforcement learning model to simulate market scenarios. During 2015-18, she worked at Citicorp as a full-stack Software Developer, where she worked in the Capital Markets Technology team, developing and maintaining an Automated Test Generation Engine for converting plaintext testing requirements to JUnit testcases using Natural Language Processing. She also worked on a Brokerage Fee prediction model, and real time processing of Big Data for auditing purposes.

During her time at Citicorp, she was a part of Prometheus, the Global Women Leadership Development program at Citi. She was also a volunteer for The National Service Scheme (NSS), an Indian government-sponsored public service program.
